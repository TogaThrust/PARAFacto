PRIVACY POLICY - PARAFACTO NATIVE SOFTWARE
Document version: 2026-04-08-02
================================================================================

1. PURPOSE
This policy explains how personal data is processed when using PARAFacto Native,
installed locally on your workstation. It is intended to support compliance with
EU Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR), Belgian law of 30 July 2018, and applicable rules
for health data and minors.

2. DATA CONTROLLER
The data controller for data entered and managed in the software is the healthcare
or paramedical professional (or legal entity) operating the software.
Unless otherwise contractually agreed, the software editor acts as a processor
limited to software provision.

3. PROCESSING DESCRIPTION
PARAFacto Native supports patient records, appointment planning, session logging,
invoicing and document generation (e.g. PDF). Data is mainly stored locally on
your device and in configured folders, except when optional features send data to
third parties (email, subscription/license services, etc.).

4. CATEGORIES OF DATA
Depending on usage, processed data may include:
- Identification/contact data (name, phone, email, internal code, etc.)
- Minor-related and family-context data where relevant
- Health data (GDPR art. 9) needed for professional follow-up
- Administrative/financial data (pricing, invoicing, payments, insurers)
- Limited technical data (timestamps, file paths, app settings)
- Subscription/license related technical identifiers

5. DATA ABOUT MINORS
When a patient is a minor, the controller must ensure lawful processing and, where
required, proper information and authorization from legal representatives.

6. PURPOSES
Data is processed for:
- Practice administration and organization
- Appointment/session planning and follow-up
- Billing and internal accounting documents
- Legal/regulatory/professional obligations
- Backup and document copy sending if configured
- License/subscription management when enabled

7. LEGAL BASES
Depending on context, legal bases may include contract, legal obligations, vital
interests, public interest in health, or explicit consent where required.

8. RECIPIENTS AND PROCESSORS
Data is generally accessible only to authorized practice members. Additional
recipients may be involved via email providers, cloud/storage tools, payment or
license related services, depending on your configuration.

9. TRANSFERS OUTSIDE EU/EEA
If your providers transfer data outside EU/EEA, you must ensure appropriate
safeguards (adequacy decision, SCCs, etc.) and provide required information.

10. RETENTION
Retention periods must follow legal/professional obligations. Typical examples:
- Invoicing/accounting data: often at least 7 years in Belgium
- Patient files: according to legal/professional requirements
- Technical acceptance logs: as needed for compliance evidence

11. SECURITY
You must implement suitable security (strong OS passwords, encrypted disk when
possible, regular backups, access control, updated antivirus and system updates).

12. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
Subject to GDPR conditions/exceptions, data subjects may request access,
rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability, consent withdrawal,
and may lodge a complaint with the Belgian DPA:
https://www.dataprotectionauthority.be

13. AUTOMATED DECISIONS
The software does not perform automated decisions producing legal/similar effects
within GDPR art. 22, unless explicitly documented otherwise.

14. CHANGES
This policy may be updated. A new in-app acceptance can be required after
substantial changes. The version shown at the top is authoritative.

15. CONTACT
For privacy questions: parafacto@parafacto.be
